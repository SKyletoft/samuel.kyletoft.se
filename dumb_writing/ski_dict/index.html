Going skiing in the alps with English speakers is a mess of mixed languages. A natural consequence of the alps not being in Britain. Here's a list of words you should know.

  • Piste (noun) [Peest]

    What beginners and Americans call a ski slope.

  • Piste (-bashing) (verb) [Peest]

  • Pomme [Pom]/[Pommeh]

    An uncomfortable one-person lift dragging you up the hill. Apparently called buttons in America.

  • Anchor

    A slightly more comfortable version of the pomme where the seat is replaced by an anchor so that two people can sit on it. Also known as T-bars.
